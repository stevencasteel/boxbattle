================================================================================ FIRST BATTLE OVERHAUL HANDOFF REPORT

This report is designed for the next AI session. The objective is to transition
the current first battle from a static arena with four fixed respawning minions
into a more robust vertical slice featuring:

  - Authored encounter pacing
  - Readable enemy roles and silhouettes
  - Stronger boss attack patterns
  - Richer projectile presentations
  - A clear technical architecture for enemy wave management and placements.

1.  CURRENT IMPLEMENTATION SNAPSHOT

The first battle is currently driven by the following files:

  - src/core/levelData.ts: Handles arena geometry, player/boss starting
    positions, and four static spawners.

  - src/entities/Spawner.ts: Each spawner immediately creates one minion, then
    respawns that same type after a hard-coded 5.0-second delay when the active
    minion is defeated.

  - src/core/Engine.ts: Creates activeSpawners from levelConfig.spawners,
    updates every spawner each fixed tick, and resets them on retry.

  - src/core/BattleDirector.ts: Handles dialogue, boss phase thresholds, and
    victory/defeat cinematics.

  - src/entities/Boss.ts & src/entities/BossStates.ts: Handles boss movement and
    attack states.

  - src/entities/BaseMinion.ts, src/entities/MinionStates.ts, & subclasses:
    Drives individual turret, lancer, and flyer behaviors.

  - src/entities/Projectile.ts & src/entities/ProjectileStrategy.ts: Handles
    pooled projectile behavior, collisions, trail rendering, and body rendering.

  - src/entities/components/FireballComponent.ts: Manages player charge shot
    timing, damage, projectile size, and firing.

  - src/core/visuals/MinionVisuals.ts & src/core/visuals/BossVisuals.ts: Renders
    simple silhouettes.

Current static spawns in use:

  - TURRET at (175, 490)
  - TURRET at (1075, 490)
  - LANCER at (625, 740)
  - FLYER at (625, 400)

The main limitation of the current encounter is the lack of pacing: spawners are
active immediately, spawners only track one minion at a time, respawn rates are
globally uniform, and the boss does not coordinate with active minion pressure.

2.  TARGET EXPERIENCE

The goal is to design an experience that resembles a polished vertical slice:

  - An opening segment that teaches the arena layout and basic boss patterns
    before adding more complex hazards.

  - Minions that appear from logical, authored "threat lanes" rather than
    constant, arbitrary respawns.

  - A boss that creates high-intensity moments, recovery windows, phase
    escalations, and memorable patterns.

  - Distinct enemy silhouettes and attacks to communicate roles clearly.

  - Projectiles that are highly readable at speed and visually differentiated by
    owner, damage, and pattern.

  - Balanced variance to keep repeated attempts engaging and fair.

  - Target Fight Length: 90 to 150 seconds for an average successful attempt.

  - Target Active Enemy Budget:

      - Phase 1: Boss + 0 to 1 active minion.
      - Phase 2: Boss + 1 to 2 active minions.
      - Phase 3: Boss + 2 to 3 active minions (during brief pressure windows).

4.  ENCOUNTER PACING PLAN

Instead of autonomous, independent spawners, the encounter will be driven by an
Encounter Director using the following pacing structure:

  - Beat: intro

      - Trigger: Fight start, 0 to 8 seconds.
      - Spawn Behavior: No minions for the first 3 seconds. Spawn exactly one
        TURRET at left-catwalk or right-catwalk after the first boss attack.
      - Boss Behavior: Restricted to single shots or basic lunges.

  - Beat: phase1-learn

      - Trigger: Boss HP between 100% and 70%.
      - Spawn Behavior: Maintain a maximum of 1 active minion. Prefer a single
        turret or lancer. Spawn cooldown: 10 to 14 seconds.
      - Boss Behavior: Uses standard single shot and lunge attacks.

  - Beat: phase2-crossfire

      - Trigger: Boss HP between 70% and 40%.
      - Spawn Behavior: Maintain a maximum of 2 active minions. Rotate between
        turret + lancer or flyer + lancer. Spawn cooldown: 7 to 11 seconds.
      - Boss Behavior: Introduces aimed volleys and lane denial attacks.

  - Beat: phase2-recovery

      - Trigger: Player HP falls to 2 or fewer, or player takes 2 hits within
        an 8-second window.
      - Spawn Behavior: Pause spawning for 6 seconds and clear queued waves.
      - Boss Behavior: Favors melee/lunge attacks over projectile spam to allow
        player recovery.

  - Beat: phase3-surge

      - Trigger: Boss HP falls below 40%.
      - Spawn Behavior: High-pressure windows (maximum of 3 minions) lasting 12
        seconds, followed by a 6-second quiet window. Spawn cooldown: 6 to 9s.
      - Boss Behavior: Adds radial burst and fan-shaped projectile attacks.

  - Beat: final-burn

      - Trigger: Boss HP falls below 15%.
      - Spawn Behavior: No new minions unless the arena is completely empty for
        8 seconds.
      - Boss Behavior: Highly active patterns to emphasize the boss encounter.

5.  ARCHITECTURE CHANGE: ENCOUNTER DIRECTOR

A new system will be introduced to coordinate these waves:

  - New File: src/core/EncounterDirector.ts

Spawn timing decisions will be shifted out of Spawner.ts. Spawner will be
treated as a low-level factory or replaced with an EnemySpawnService.

Recommended Types in src/core/levelData.ts:

export interface SpawnAnchor {
  id: string;
  x: number;
  y: number;
  tags: string[];
}

export interface EncounterWave {
  id: string;
  phase: 1 | 2 | 3;
  minBossHpPct?: number;
  maxBossHpPct?: number;
  earliestTime?: number;
  cooldownRange: [number, number];
  maxActiveMinions: number;
  entries: EncounterSpawnEntry[];
}

export interface EncounterSpawnEntry {
  type: MinionType;
  anchorTags?: string[];
  anchorIds?: string[];
  weight: number;
  maxAliveOfType?: number;
}

EncounterDirector Responsibilities:

1.  Track total elapsed fight time.
2.  Monitor boss HP ratio and player HP status.
3.  Enforce a phase-based active minion budget.
4.  Select valid encounter waves and spawn anchors.
5.  Enforce safety checks (minimum player/boss distances, hazard avoidance, and
    per-type minion limits).
6.  Perform weighted random selections using TrigLUT.randomGameplay() to ensure
    determinism within the gameplay RNG.
7.  Publish events (e.g., ENCOUNTER_BEAT_STARTED, MINION_SPAWNED) for feedback,
    SFX, or debugging systems.

Engine Integration Steps:

  - In Engine.init: Instantiate encounterDirector.
  - In Engine.fixedUpdate: Update the director after battleDirector and before
    minion collision systems.
  - On Reset/Retry: Invoke encounterDirector.reset() to clear state.
  - Cleanup: Ensure active minions are cleaned up properly during transitions.

Modifications to Spawner.ts:

  - Remove automatic spawning in the constructor.
  - Remove the hard-coded respawn delay.
  - Expose a factory method such as spawnMinion(type, position, world), or
    delegate this to a dedicated src/entities/MinionFactory.ts.

7.  BOSS IMPROVEMENTS

Adjustments to Projectile Counts (to be configured as constants in
src/entities/BossAttackPatterns.ts rather than inline literals):

  - Radial Omni Burst:

      - Phase 1: 8 projectiles (existing).
      - Phase 2: Increase to 12 projectiles.
      - Phase 3: Increase to 16 projectiles.

  - Boss Volley attacks:

      - Phase 2: Increase from 3 to 4 shots, with a spacing of 0.18s.
      - Phase 3: Increase from 5 to 7 shots, with a spacing of 0.14s.

New Attack Patterns:

  - FAN_BURST: Spawns 5 to 9 projectiles in an arc centered on the player's
    direction, using a spread of 50 to 80 degrees.
  - PREDICTIVE_SHOT: Fires at the estimated position of the player after 0.35
    seconds based on player velocity. Clamp the lead distance so it remains
    dodgeable.
  - GROUND_SWEEP: Low horizontal projectile or shockwave launched at the boss's
    feet, requiring the player to jump.
  - RING_WITH_GAP: A radial projectile burst containing a single open gap
    oriented away from the player's natural escape route.
  - LUNGE_CHAIN: In Phase 3, links a short lunge with a retreat hop or fan burst
    rather than repeated direct lunges.

AI Decision-Making Upgrades:

  - Implement an attack selection utility scorer. Score options based on player
    distance, relative height, recent attack history, phase, active minion
    pressure, and player health.
  - Introduce cooldown tags for attack categories (e.g., 'projectile-heavy',
    'melee', 'arena-denial').
  - Use a short attack history ring buffer to prevent repetitive patterns.
  - Build in explicit recovery windows following high-intensity phases to give
    the fight a natural rhythm.
  - Read player state context (e.g., if player is airborne, favor fan bursts; if
    player is close and grounded, favor lunges or ground sweeps; if player is
    low health, prioritize spectacle over minion pressure).

8.  MINION IMPROVEMENTS

  - Lancer (Close-Range Charger):

      - Render an active lance graphic in MinionVisuals.draw when active.
      - Establish an active attack hitbox during LancerAttackState extending
        beyond the body.
      - Clearer telegraph: Lance pulls back and glows yellow before lunging
        forward.
      - Implement basic edge detection so lancers halt or turn back at platform
        edges rather than falling off.
      - Implementation details: Use a specialized attack hitbox (width 90,
        height 18, horizontal offset facingDirection * 55, vertical offset -24)
        that inflicts standard damage but has increased knockback.

  - Turret (Stationary Shooter):

      - Render a rotating barrel pointing toward the player's coordinates.
      - Implement standard line-of-sight checks against solid terrain before
        firing.
      - Add a phase 3 burst variant: 2 rapid shots with a minor aim adjustment
        between them.
      - Set a minimum-range dead zone where the turret cannot fire, allowing
        aggressive close-range play.

  - Flyer (Airborne Patrol):

      - Make patrol paths data-driven using spawn anchor coordinates instead of
        a fixed vertical offset.
      - Introduce a strafe state where the flyer moves horizontally while
        firing.
      - Introduce a dive-bomb attack with clear visual telegraphs and recovery
        stiffness in Phase 3.
      - Marginally reduce projectile speed when firing on the move for
        readability.

9.  NEW MINION TYPE: SHIELDER

One new support type will be introduced in the first pass:

  - Role: A slow-moving support minion that projects a forward-facing shield arc
    oriented toward the player.

  - Gameplay Purpose: Forces the player to reposition, jump, or utilize charge
    shots.

  - Behavior: Patrols slowly between nearby anchors, faces the player, and
    periodically braces.

  - Vulnerability: Vulnerable from behind; charged shots pierce its shield.

13. CONCRETE ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

The implementation is verified when:

  - No minions spawn during the first 3 seconds of the encounter.
  - Phase 1 active minion count is capped at 1.
  - Phase 2 active minion count remains at 1-2, rotating between multiple spawn
    anchor locations.
  - Phase 3 spawns waves of up to 3 minions during designated pressure windows,
    separated by readable recovery periods.
  - Lancers render a visible weapon and apply damage/knockback via the lance
    hitbox.
  - Boss Phase 3 features at least one pattern releasing 12 or more projectiles
    with clear, readable dodge paths.
  - The player's charged shot is visually distinct from standard shots and
    clears enemy projectiles readably.
  - Projectile pool is stable under maximum load, and npm run build succeeds.
